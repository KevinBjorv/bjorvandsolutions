First-Person Puzzle Game

Every room has a forbidden button. Every exit is on the other side of pressing it.

Set inside the Museum of Forbidden Buttons, this bright first-person puzzle game drops you into a minimalist chain of self-contained puzzle rooms where one bad decision becomes the entire puzzle.

A single press rewrites the rules of the room. Freeze gravity, make everything bounce, or throw an object and teleport to it. Use each shift to cross gaps, redirect objects, outsmart hazards, and reach the exit.

What It Is

A calm museum, a bad idea in the middle of the room, and a puzzle that starts after you press it.

Forbidden Buttons stays focused on inventive rule changes, readable puzzle design, and the fun of breaking a room wide open. The rooms are compact, self-contained, and built for clean, satisfying aha moments instead of slow friction.

Built for readable puzzle spaces

Short chambers, strong feedback, and quick resets keep experimentation sharp instead of muddy.

Minimalist on the surface

Clean visuals keep the rule change easy to understand the moment the room turns against you.

Wrong underneath

Beneath the museum’s polished facade sits a corporate testing program that feels increasingly off.

Rule Changes

Three standout ways to break the room.

Every chamber is built around a dramatic shift that instantly changes how the puzzle works. Pick a rule change below to preview the vibe.

Freeze gravity

A single press rewrites vertical logic. Suddenly the solution is about seeing the same room under a new rule instead of looking for a hidden trick.

  • Use the rule change to create new paths across the room.
  • Read the space fast and restart instantly when an attempt fails.
  • Keep the focus on the aha moment, not on recovering from the mistake.

Make everything bounce

Reliable surfaces stop being reliable. You need to redirect movement, think about impact, and solve the room through controlled chaos instead of direct lines.

  • Redirect objects and movement instead of walking a simple route.
  • Turn hazards and geometry into part of the puzzle logic.
  • Experiment rapidly until the room clicks into place.

Throwport by teleporting to objects you throw

Throw an object, then jump to it. It is a clean, readable mechanic that opens the room from angles that should not be available a few seconds earlier.

  • Cross gaps and reposition yourself in a single decisive move.
  • Chain experiments quickly without getting stuck in long resets.
  • Break a room wide open with a rule that feels instantly understandable.
